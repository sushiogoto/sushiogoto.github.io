Nepal needs your help.

On April 25th, the worst earthquake in over 80 years struck Nepal, killing over 7000 people. The death toll continues to rise. Hundreds of thousands of people have been left homeless. Aftershocks continue to rock the country, further damaging already weakened infrastructure and buildings. People need your help. From Friday(8th May) and Saturday(9th May), 11am to 4pm Uber will come and pickup your donations, free of charge, and we will get them into the hands of those who need help.

Donation Information

This campaign will only run from Friday 8th May and Saturday 9th May, 11am to 4pm.

We are working with Childreach Nepal who are on the ground and have identified these as the most critical in need items to help the people of Nepal. Also, the airport in Kathmandu is already operating at maximum capacity. Anything sent that is not critical, will prevent critical items from entering the country. If you have none of these, please consider donating money.

Please ensure items are undamaged and durable.

We are only accepting the following donations, items not on the list will not be accepted.


Top Priorities

  • Tents (帳篷)
  • Tarpaulins (篷布) (Preferably 12ft x 18ft)
  • Water Purifiers (淨水器)
  • Water Purification Tablets (淨水藥片)
  • Monetary Donations (金錢捐贈)*

Secondary Priorities

  • NEW Blankets (毯子)**
  • Basic cooking utensils (基本炊具)
  • Solar Lanterns (太陽能燈)

* To make a monetary donation, order an UberAID vehicle and tell the driver you would like to make a monetary donation. The driver will ask the amount and input the HKD amount for you. Uber will follow up in the next month with a tax-refundable receipt. You do not need to donate items to make a monetary donation.

** The people of Nepal will be at high risk of disease outbreak, we cannot risk sending them contaminated blankets.

Who will receive the donations?

We are working with Childreach Nepal who have always worked in the rural, mountainous regions of Nepal, and these have been hit especially hard. Two of the villages they work in, Shermathang and Manikharka in Sindupalchok, have been completely destroyed. They have yet to hear from their other villages.

Their staff and trustees in Kathmandu have been working around the clock to get clothing, food and medical supplies to those stranded in the rural villages.
